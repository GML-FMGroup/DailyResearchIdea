# Probabilistic Occlusion-Aware Depth for Robust Monocular Novel View Synthesis

## Motivation

MetaView relies on monocular depth estimation which fails under extreme occlusions, leading to distorted geometry in novel views. Depth Anything 3 demonstrates strong geometry but still assumes known or estimated poses; occlusion remains unmodeled. The root cause is that a single image provides ambiguous depth in occluded regions, and existing methods treat all depth predictions with equal confidence, propagating errors.

## Key Insight

Explicitly modeling the uncertainty in depth estimates via per-pixel distributions and occlusion masks allows the view synthesis process to downweight unreliable regions, thus improving robustness to occlusion-induced artifacts.

## Method

**(A) What it is:** Probabilistic Occlusion-Aware Depth Fusion (POADF) is a module that, given a single image, predicts a per-pixel depth distribution (mean and variance) and a binary occlusion mask, then outputs a confidence-weighted depth map. This depth map serves as geometry prior for a diffusion-based novel view synthesizer (e.g., MetaView).

**(B) How it works:**
```python
# Pseudocode for POADF forward pass
import torch

def poadf(image):
    # image: [B,3,H,W]
    # Feature extraction shared backbone
    features = shared_encoder(image)  # e.g., DINO pre-trained
    
    # Depth distribution head: predicts mean and log variance
    depth_mean = depth_head(features)  # [B,1,H,W]
    depth_log_var = depth_head_var(features)  # [B,1,H,W]
    
    # Occlusion mask head: predicts probability of being occluded
    occ_logits = occlusion_head(features)  # [B,1,H,W]
    occ_mask = torch.sigmoid(occ_logits)  # [B,1,H,W]
    
    # Confidence-weighted depth: use inverse variance and (1 - occ_mask)
    confidence = (1 - occ_mask) * torch.exp(-depth_log_var)  # higher is more reliable
    depth_weighted = depth_mean * confidence
    # Normalize? No, keep as is for downstream conditioning
    return depth_weighted, confidence, depth_mean, depth_log_var, occ_mask
```

The confidence-weighted depth is concatenated with the input image as conditioning to a diffusion model (MetaView's denoising U-Net). The diffusion loss includes an additional term: a KL divergence between predicted depth distribution and a Laplace distribution centered at ground truth depth for pixels that are not occluded (occ_mask = 0), and a separate loss to encourage occluded pixels to have high uncertainty.

**(C) Why this design:** We chose to predict depth as a Gaussian distribution (mean + log variance) over a deterministic depth because it allows the model to express uncertainty naturally, which is crucial for occluded regions where depth is ambiguous. We selected a binary occlusion head over a continuous occlusion probability because it simplifies integration: (1 - occ_mask) directly scales confidence, and the thresholded mask can be supervised with ground-truth occlusion labels (e.g., from synthetic data or ray-traced visibility). We opted for a shared encoder (DINO) rather than a task-specific CNN because it provides strong semantic features that generalize across scenes; the trade-off is that the encoder may not be optimal for depth-specific detail, but we mitigate this by fine-tuning the heads. We chose to multiply inverse variance with (1-occ_mask) rather than using variance alone because occlusion is a binary event that can be learned from visibility cues, whereas variance captures aleatoric uncertainty from textureless regions; combining both provides a more reliable confidence signal. The cost is that the occlusion head requires supervised data, which may be hard to obtain for real images; we address this by using synthetic data (from CAD models) with ground-truth depth and visibility, then fine-tune on real data with pseudo-labels generated by cross-view consistency.

**(D) Why it measures what we claim:** The computational quantity `confidence = (1 - occ_mask) * exp(-depth_log_var)` measures the reliability of the depth estimate for novel view synthesis because we assume that occluded pixels (occ_mask=1) have no valid depth in the source view, and that high variance (exp(-depth_log_var) small) indicates high ambiguity in depth prediction. This assumption fails when the occlusion mask is inaccurate (e.g., due to domain gap in training data), in which case confidence may incorrectly suppress reliable depth or fail to suppress unreliable depth. The quantity `depth_log_var` measures aleatoric uncertainty because we assume that the model learns to assign high variance to pixels where the input image does not constrain depth (e.g., textureless or homogeneous regions); this assumption fails for regions with repeated texture where the model may falsely be confident, in which case variance reflects epistemic uncertainty instead. By using confidence to weight the depth map, we ensure that the diffusion model places less emphasis on unreliable depth during synthesis, directly addressing the motivation that monocular depth fails under occlusions. The KL divergence loss on non-occluded pixels ensures the depth mean is accurate where we have evidence, while the absence of loss on occluded pixels allows the model to freely increase variance, aligning with the notion that occluded regions are inherently uncertain.

## Contribution

(1) A probabilistic depth estimation module that jointly predicts per-pixel depth distributions and occlusion masks, producing a confidence-weighted depth map. (2) A training strategy that leverages synthetic data with ground-truth occlusion labels and fine-tunes on real data with pseudo-labels from cross-view consistency. (3) Empirical demonstration that modeling occlusion uncertainty via confidence weighting improves novel view synthesis quality under large viewpoint changes compared to deterministic depth priors.

## Experiment

### Evaluation Setup

| Role | Choice | Rationale (≤12 words) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Dataset | ShapeNet-NVS + RealEstate10K | Synthetic with GT depth & occlusion; real for generalization |
| Primary metric | PSNR (overall and occluded) | Measures synthesis quality; occluded subset tests claim |
| Baseline 1 | MetaView (no depth prior) | Baseline without geometry guidance |
| Baseline 2 | Depth Anything (monocular depth) | Strong depth prior without fusion |
| Baseline 3 | Deterministic Depth Fusion (ours w/o uncertainty) | Ablates uncertainty and occlusion modeling |
| Ablation-of-ours | Ours without occlusion mask | Removes occlusion-awareness, only variance |

### Why this setup validates the claim
This setup tests each sub-claim: MetaView checks whether injecting any depth prior helps synthesis; Depth Anything checks whether probabilistic fusion outperforms raw monocular depth; the deterministic ablation isolates the benefit of uncertainty and occlusion modeling; and the occlusion-free ablation isolates the occlusion mask's contribution. PSNR on occluded regions is the critical metric because the central claim is that confidence-weighted depth specifically improves regions with occlusion. If our method improves overall PSNR but not on occluded pixels, the claim fails. Conversely, gains confined to occluded areas would confirm the mechanism.

### Expected outcome and causal chain

**vs. MetaView** — On a case with large viewpoint change causing significant occlusion, MetaView without depth prior produces blurry or hallucinated content in occluded regions because it has no geometry guidance. Our method uses confidence-weighted depth, which suppresses unreliable depth in occluded areas, forcing the diffusion to rely more on visible context, leading to sharper and more consistent synthesis. We expect a noticeable improvement in PSNR on occluded pixels (e.g., 1–2 dB) while parity on non-occluded.

**vs. Depth Anything** — On a case with textureless regions or repeated patterns, Depth Anything may output confident but wrong depth, leading to distorted geometry when back-projected. Our method predicts high variance for such ambiguous regions, reducing the weight, and the occlusion mask further suppresses if the pixel is occluded. We expect better PSNR overall, specifically on textureless areas where variance reduces artifacts (e.g., 0.5–1 dB).

**vs. Deterministic Depth Fusion** — On a case where occlusion is present, deterministic fusion blindly uses incorrect depth for occluded pixels, causing mapping artifacts. Our method's occlusion mask and variance weighting avoid this. We expect a clear gap in PSNR on occluded regions between ours and the deterministic version (e.g., 2–3 dB).

### What would falsify this idea
If our method does not show a larger improvement on occluded regions compared to non-occluded, or if the occlusion-mask ablation shows no difference on occluded areas, then the central claim that occlusion-aware confidence weighting specifically improves synthesis for occlusions is false.
